Always maintain proper observation

Maintaining proper observation is one of the most important skills every driver should develop. This means consistently checking your mirrors, scanning the road ahead, and being aware of vehicles, pedestrians, and potential hazards around you. Good observation helps you anticipate the actions of other road users and respond safely to changing traffic conditions. By staying alert and aware at all times, drivers can avoid sudden surprises, make better decisions, and significantly reduce the risk of accidents.

  • Be especially alert at intersections, crossings, and roundabouts, where multiple vehicles, pedestrians, and cyclists may appear unexpectedly.
  • Observe the behavior of surrounding drivers, such as speed changes or lane positioning, to anticipate sudden movements and react smoothly.
  • Scan the road far ahead, not just in front of your vehicle, allowing you to identify traffic signals, pedestrians, road signs, and potential hazards early.
  • Eliminate distractions and maintain concentration, as observation depends on staying focused and ready to respond to changing road conditions at all times.
  • Check your mirrors regularly, including rear-view and side mirrors, to maintain awareness of vehicles approaching from behind or traveling alongside you.
